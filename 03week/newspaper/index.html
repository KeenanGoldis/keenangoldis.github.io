SK Expat Creates New Smart-Phone App

Keenan Goldis never would have thought after barely graduating from South Kingstown High School that we would one day create an app that would make him a multi-millionaire in his early thirties. After opening up and running an artisanal sausage food trailer, he decided to shut down his business after concluding that it was not a profitable enough business venture. He then studied accounting and became an accountant at the Texas State Securities Board. He quickly realized that, much like a bear who has tasted human blood, so too must he start another business venture.

That's when found Austin Coding Academy, which offers a nine-month program that teaches students all the skills necessary to start a career as a Web Developer. Former classmate John Doe describes Goldis as: "Kind of weird. He themed most of his web-pages about sausages. He would make analogies comparing writing code to processing meat, and I always just smiled and nodded because I didn't know what he was talking about."

After graduating from Austin Coding Academy in September, 2017, he spent two years working at the Austin-based start-up that made apps for local businesses. This gave Goldis a diverse experience working with many different clients and building different types of smart phone apps. This is where Goldis came up with his idea to create Goldis Green, which is an app that diagnoses the health garden plants by analyzing pictures.

Just this last July, Goldis sold his app, which now has over 10,000 downloads to Facebook for $100,000,000,000. When asked about his good fortune Goldis says:"The best way to get rich is to make an app and hope that Facebook pays you a completely ridiculous amount of money for it."
